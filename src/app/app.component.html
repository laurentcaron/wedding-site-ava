The Sofreh Aghd

A Persian tradition

A meaningful part of our ceremony is the Sofreh Aghd, a traditional Persian wedding spread rich in symbolism and beauty. Carefully arranged, it reflects wishes for a marriage filled with love, prosperity, and harmony. Each element placed upon it carries significance, honoring both heritage and the journey ahead.

Symbolic Elements

Mirror & Candles Aayeneh · Shamdoon

Light, clarity, and the reflection of a shared future.

Honey Asal

Sweetness — the couple shares honey to mark the beginning of a life filled with kindness and joy.

Bread, Cheese & Herbs Nan o Panir Sabzi

A blessing for prosperity, sustenance, and a life rich in simple pleasures.

Decorated Eggs Tokhmeh Morgh

Fertility and new beginnings.

Nuts & Sweets Ajil

A symbol of abundance and a sweet life together.

Coins Sekkeh

Signifying wealth and financial prosperity.

Sacred Text or Poetry Book

Often a volume of Persian poetry or a religious book — representing spirituality, wisdom, and guidance.

Sugar Cones Kalleh Ghand

Gently ground above the couple during the ceremony, showering them in sweetness and blessings.

Rosewater Golab

Purity and fragrance — a sense of calm and grace to the union.

This timeless tradition is a reflection of culture, intention, and love — woven into the very beginning of our marriage. We are honored to share it with you.
